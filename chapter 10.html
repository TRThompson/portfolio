Chapter 10 - Computer systems

KEY POINTS:
The CPU carries out all instructions in the computer
CPU is made of billions of transistors
Clock speed = the operating speed of a computer or its microprocessor, expressed in cycles per second. Measured in gigahertz
Fetch - decode - execute cycle
CPU
Brain of the computer - carries out the instructions given to it
Inside a processor there is billions of transistors (electronic switches) that can be combined to build the circuitry inside a processor.
Moore’s Law: number of transistors on a processor would double approximately every 2 years
Gordon Moore is founder of intel (one of world’s best-known processor companies)
He predicted in 1965 the number of transistors on a processor would double approximately every 2 years - this has been true until recently when we are struggling with physical limits
Doubling the number of transistors in the same space increases speed of the processor - processor speed has increased exponentially (Smartphones today have faster processors than desktop computers 10 years ago)
Processor runs to the timings of a clock signal - clock speed is measured in hertz
